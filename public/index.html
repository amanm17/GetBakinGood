How it works (quick)

Device conversions

Convection/fan and air fryers move hot air aggressively, so they often need lower temperature and/or shorter time.

  • Convection: typically lower temperature by about 14°C (25°F) and/or reduce time (often up to ~25%).
  • Air fryer: commonly reduce temperature a little and reduce time by about ~20%.
  • Convection microwave (convection mode): similar idea; smaller cavity can cook faster for roasts.

Real‑world factors

  • Quantity scales time non‑linearly (doubling ≠ doubling time).
  • Thickness matters more than weight for meats/fish.
  • Cookware affects heat transfer and browning (glass/ceramic vs metal).
  • Airflow: overcrowding slows cooking in convection/air fryers.
